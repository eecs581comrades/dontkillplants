What plant is right for you?

Source: Liu, Y., Xu, X., Dimitrov, D. et al. An updated floristic map of the world. Nat Commun 14, 2990 (2023).

Source: USDA

When deciding what plant is right for you, there are several factors to keep in mind.

The most important part is where you are located in the world - your availability of plants will heavily depend on what plants are native to your region. We encourage you to primarily focus on growing native plants, as they have a better likelihood of surviving.

In the United States, Canada, Denmark, and France, the United States Department of Agriculture has a native plants database that may prove useful to outdoor planting. Just input your location!

However, if you're just looking for houseplants, read on.

Source: Unsplash

Dry Areas

Dry areas require drought-resistant plants, such as Rosemary, Sage and California Lilac. A partial list can be found from the California Water Service. When buying drought-resistant plants, remember not to water them too often - they were meant to go weeks on end without water.

Source: Unsplash

Cold Areas

Cold areas require temperature-resistant plants, such as Lily-of-the-Valley, Coneflower, and Holly. A partial list can be found from Shoal Creek Nursery. Remember to keep cold-resistant plants at a cooler temperature while still providing lots of sun.

Source: Unsplash

Windy areas

Windy areas require wind-resistant plants, such as Jerusalem Sage, Italian Aster, and Lamb's ear. A partial list can be found from the Royal Horticultural Society. Remember to give wind-resistant plants lots of dirt to grow their roots in.
